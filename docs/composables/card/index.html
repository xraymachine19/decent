Examples

Basic cards

Cards give children elements uniform spacing and have a max-width of an aside:

Welcome to the club.

Don't forget to validate parking!

<div class="card">
  <h3>Welcome to the club.</h3>
  <p>Don't forget to validate parking!</p>
</div>

Elements in cards bleed to the edges, so headlines with color classes will look like this:

Welcome to the club.

Don't forget to validate parking!

<div class="card">
  <h3 class="info">Welcome to the club.</h3>
  <p>Don't forget to validate parking!</p>
</div>

Cards support the other composables, so you can make them into a matte or well:

Welcome to the club.

Don't forget to validate parking!

Welcome to the club.

Don't forget to validate parking!

<div class="card well">
  <h3>Welcome to the club.</h3>
  <p>Don't forget to validate parking!</p>
</div>
<div class="card matte">
  <h3>Welcome to the club.</h3>
  <p>Don't forget to validate parking!</p>
</div>

Advanced cards

Card layout

In general, cards stack their child elements. But Decent's layout composables can be used to achieve more complicated layouts:

The Mouser

If there's a rat about, the criminal cat gang goes to The Mouser.

Fuzzy M.

Featuring content

Cards with an img or .overlay component up front will feature it, making it fill the full width of the card. The overlay component can be good for putting information or buttons in useful spots on the feature image.

Dog

His name is ironic or something. Don't cross him. His meow is worse than his bite.

Scratches

Nobody knows if his name refers to the scratches that cover his body, or those he's dished out to others. Everyone knows better than to ask.
